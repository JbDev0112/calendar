All Soul's Day

Remembering and Honoring the Departed

Overview

All Soul's Day, observed on November 2nd, is a Christian tradition dedicated to remembering and praying for the souls of the departed. It follows All Saint's Day and focuses on honoring all faithful Christians who have passed away, particularly those believed to be in purgatory. The day is marked by various customs, including attending church services, lighting candles, and visiting cemeteries to clean and decorate graves with flowers. It is a time for reflection on mortality, the afterlife, and the hope of resurrection. All Soul's Day serves as a reminder of the spiritual connection between the living and the dead, encouraging prayers for the souls' peaceful rest and eternal happiness.
