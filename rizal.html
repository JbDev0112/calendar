Rizal Day

Honoring the Legacy of Dr. José Rizal

Overview

Rizal Day is a national holiday in the Philippines celebrated on December 30th each year. It commemorates the life and works of Dr. José Rizal, a Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary who is widely regarded as the national hero of the Philippines. Rizal's writings, particularly his novels "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," played a crucial role in inspiring the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. He was executed by the Spanish authorities on December 30, 1896, for his involvement in the movement for independence. Rizal Day serves as a reminder of his contributions to Philippine history and his enduring legacy in the fight for freedom and justice. The holiday is marked by various ceremonies, including wreath-laying at Rizal's monument in Luneta Park, educational activities, and cultural events that celebrate his life and ideals.
