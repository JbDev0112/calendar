All Saint's Day

A Celebration of All the Saints and Souls

Overview

All Saint's Day, also known as All Hallows' Day, is a Christian festival celebrated on November 1st each year. It is a day dedicated to honoring all the saints, both known and unknown, who have attained heaven. The holiday has its roots in the early Christian tradition of commemorating martyrs and saints who have made significant contributions to the faith. All Saint's Day is observed with various customs and traditions around the world, including attending church services, lighting candles, and visiting cemeteries to pay respects to deceased loved ones. In many cultures, it is a time for reflection, prayer, and celebration of the spiritual legacy left by the saints. The day is often followed by All Soul's Day on November 2nd, which specifically honors the souls of the departed.
