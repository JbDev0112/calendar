Independence Day

Celebrating Freedom and National Pride

Overview

Independence Day, commonly known as the Fourth of July in the United States, is a federal holiday that commemorates the adoption of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776. This historic document marked the birth of the nation and its separation from British rule. Independence Day is celebrated with a variety of festivities, including fireworks displays, parades, barbecues, concerts, and patriotic ceremonies. It is a day for Americans to express their national pride, reflect on the values of freedom and democracy, and spend time with family and friends. The holiday is also an opportunity to honor the sacrifices made by those who fought for the country's independence and to celebrate the diverse cultural heritage that defines the nation.
