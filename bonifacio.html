Bonifacio Day

Honoring the Legacy of Andrés Bonifacio

Overview

Bonifacio Day is a national holiday in the Philippines celebrated on November 30th each year. It honors the birth anniversary of Andrés Bonifacio, a Filipino revolutionary leader and the founder of the Katipunan, a secret society that played a crucial role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. Bonifacio is often referred to as the "Father of the Philippine Revolution" and is considered one of the country's national heroes. The holiday serves as a reminder of Bonifacio's contributions to the fight for independence and his enduring legacy in Philippine history. Celebrations typically include ceremonies, parades, and educational activities that highlight Bonifacio's life and achievements.
