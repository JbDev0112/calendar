Black Saturday

Holy Week

Overview

Black Saturday, also known as Holy Saturday, is the day observed between Good Friday and Easter Sunday in the Christian Holy Week. It commemorates the time Jesus Christ’s body lay in the tomb after his crucifixion and before his resurrection. This day is marked by solemn reflection, prayer, and waiting, symbolizing a period of mourning and hope. Many Christian traditions hold vigil services on Black Saturday night, anticipating the joy of Easter morning and the celebration of Jesus’ resurrection, which signifies victory over death and the promise of eternal life.
